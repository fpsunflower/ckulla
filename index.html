He previously worked at Sony Picture Imageworks on the in-house branch of the Arnold renderer for 13 years, making contributions to ray-tracing kernels, sampling techniques and volume rendering. In 2017 he was recognized with a Scientific and Engineering Award from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences for his work on the Arnold renderer.
